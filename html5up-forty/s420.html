S420

This academic project called "Sailing simulator" is a sailboat simulator which retransmits the sensations of displacement as if we were really in the open sea.

  • Learn more

The origin of the project

The source of this project is a french entrepreneur, ingenious and passionate about sailing. This man, living and working in the south of Seine-et-Marne had no possibility of encountering varied sailing conditions worthy of the high seas, he decided to build and have built a navigation device, more precisely a dinghy simulator to promote the training of skippers and others pilots in varied weather conditions while staying at home.

A short summary

The project took shape, the simulator was built, tests and uses were carried out with the inevitable imperfections or limitations of the machine. Subsequently, our entrepreneur always in search of new sensations then became passionate for aeronautics and has embarked on the training of airline pilots, thus abandoning the navigation, thus placing his "mechanical sailboat" at the landfill.
During 2014, the shipyard located in the town of Thomery just on the other side of the Seine, in front of the Lycée La Fayette lets teachers know that a "mechanical sailboat Was abandoned, so the teachers recovered it to avoid throwing it away. After further research, this "mechanical sailboat" was in truth a "dinghy type S420 ”mounted on a complex metal frame, this frame is itself piloted to recreate the conditions and sensations of navigation but in staying on earth.

Lycée La Fayette which had already participated in the development and construction in the past of several elements of this model remained very interested in this dinghy, but this This time, this dinghy would be used for the training of the students of the Lycée La Fayette. In on the other hand, the mechanics and technology used for the simulator remain current, its appearance and the bulk it generates make it, however, obsolete, it is why a return to the workshop was inevitable Thus, the dinghy was placed in the hall of the workshops of the Lycée La Fayette de Champagne on Seine in July 2014. Installed in a corner of the workshop, the simulator will be reborn.

The model could be summarized as follows: it is about a real dinghy mounted on a structure allowing movements of the hull along three perpendicular axes, in three dimensions.

My personal tasks

  • Identify the boat's position on Unity
  • Display of values
  • Convert Unity units to GPS coordinates
  • Transmit values ​​using ModBus communication protocol

A "GameObject" can represent a character, a scene or another prop on Unity, it always has a Transform component to have information on the position, the rotation and the size of an object.

It will be necessary to access the following two fields, therefore X and Z. for this it is necessary to go through the Transform function as well as the desired control to access.
Once in possession of these two data, using ModBus Protocol i was able to transmit this data to a mobile application.

Modbus is a protocol for communication used for networks programmable controllers. The protocol Modbus can be used in two ways different.

  • In RTU (RS-232; RS-422): A single active master, several passive slaves. This is the master who must read and write to each slave. RTU mode consists of of frames containing the slave number, the function to be processed (read / write), the data and the verification code called CRC16.
  • In TCP (Ethernet): Several active clients, one passive server, each client reads and writes in the server. TCP mode is made up of frames containing the function to be processed (read / write) and the data.
We used the ModBus TCP / IP protocol because we want to have a server that accepts multiple clients. Once the data transfer has been completed and validated I was able to move forward. The next step was to convert the unity data into GPS coordinates.

My proposals to the project

In a second part of the project, I had to show my creativity and what it could be add to the project. My decisions focused on functionalities that could better situate the user on the simulation.

  • Minimap
  • Horizontal Compass Hud

The mini-map is one of the essential tools of current simulators and video games, it allows the user to have a precise idea of ​​where he is in the simulation, it could be a kind of help to the user if they find themselves lost or about to encounter an unwanted obstacle and thus get around it.

Minimap display :

The horizontal compass is what we could understand as an alternative to a fixed compass, inspired by current video games, I decided to integrate a horizontal compass in the simulation. The advantages of this compass are that it saves space and, above all, the user does not need to take his eyes off his course at any time to know his position and his heading, it can be a very interesting alternative to the compass or a complement to it.
